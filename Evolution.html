The evolution of video game consals
  • 1972 – Magnavox Odyssey
      Brought familyes together because they could play together as a family.

  • 1979 – Intellivision
      Atari changed console gaming forever in 1987 by creating the first popular console capable of accepting game cartridges. it did offer competitive audio effects and graphics.

  • 1982 – ColecoVision
      Atari changed console gaming forever in 1987 by creating the first popular console capable of accepting game cartridges.The Coleco also offered expansion modules that allowed gamers to play popular games from Atari, racing games, and more.

  • 1985 – Nintendo Entertainment System
      Atari changed console gaming forever in 1987 by creating the first popular console capable of accepting game cartridges.Super Mario Bros. launched the console into popularity.

  • 1987 – Atari VCS 2600
      Atari changed console gaming forever in 1987 by creating the first popular console capable of accepting game cartridges.

  • 1989 – Sega Genesis
      ega launched the Mega Drive console in 1988 in Japan.

  • 1990 – Super Nintendo Entertainment System
      The Super Nintendo boasted upgraded 16-bit graphics over the original NES and even survived as 32-bit games became popular

  • 1993 – Mega Drive II (Genesis)
      It was sold as the Mega Drive II in Japan and simply “Genesis” in the US

  • 1995 – PlayStation
      In 1995, the Sony PlayStation took the video game market by storm, selling out 100k units in presale and over 7 million by 1996

  • 1996 – Nintendo 64
      The Nintendo 64 was one of the first game consoles to use a 64-bit processor, which gave gamers better graphics than ever before from a Nintendo system

  • 2000 – PlayStation 2
      In 2000, Sony upped their game yet again with the PlayStation 2. The new console featured DVD playback abilities, which was a huge selling point at the time.

  • 2001 – Xbox
      In 2001, Microsoft hopping into the console realm and kicked off what’s known as the “console wars.” To compete with PlayStation

  • 2005 – Xbox 360
      In continuation of the console wars, Microsoft threw another jab at Sony with the Xbox 360 in 2005

  • 2006 – Nintendo Wii
      In the heat of the console war between Sony and Xbox, Nintendo released a family-friendly alternative

  • 2006 – PlayStation 3
      Sony released the PS3 as a competitor to the Xbox 360 in 2006

  • 2013 – Xbox One
      Microsoft Xbox released their current generation of consoles in 2013 — the Xbox One. Like the Xbox 360, it continued to offer Xbox Live and third-party streaming.

  • 2013 – PlayStation 4
      It has sharp graphics, VR capabilities, and an impressive library of games to choose from

  • 2016 – Xbox One S
      In 2016, Microsoft gave the Xbox One some major upgrades with the new Xbox One S. The updated console was smaller, could upscale games from 1080p to 4K, and stream 4K video.

  • 2016 – PlayStation 4 Pro
      PlayStation 4 Pro was smaller, sleeker, and could also handle 4K upscaling.

  • 2017 – Xbox One X
      The new Xbox One X supports 4k video and gameplay natively. It also boasts 40% more processing power than any other console on the market.

  • 2017 – Nintendo Switch
      Nintendo changed the game in 2017 by offering a console that could do more. The new Nintendo Switch works as both a handheld console and a TV-based console, and the clip-in controllers give gamers a huge amount of flexibility.

  • 2020 – PlayStation 5
      The console’s Tempest 3D AudioTech affords it the capability to play hundreds of sound sources simultaneously
